The backbone, designed around the four daily jobs

Everything below exists to serve what the college actually does every day. We lock this shape before writing model code — so the foundation stops shifting.

1 Attendance · semester-wise 2 Student records 3 Faculty records 4 Internal marks · per semester

The one idea that shapes everything

Attendance and marks are per semester (Odd / Even within a year). So the Semester is the hub — every time-bound record points at it. And a student's year+section is a yearly sticker (an Enrollment), not a fixed field — which is what makes promotion and history work.

The four jobs, flowing through it

Every daily action is a short path across the boxes above.

Mark attendancejob 1 · daily
Teacher opens their assignment→ Section + Subject + date→ tap present / absent→ Attendance rows @ active Semester
Enter internal marksjob 4 · per assessment
Teacher picks Section + Subject→ choose IA1 / IA2 / Model→ enter each student's mark→ InternalMark rows @ active Semester
Add a studentjob 2 · any year
account: name, register no…→ pick Degree + Branch (= Program)→ optional: put in Section III·A→ Enrollment @ active year
Promote at year-endrollover
select Section II·A→ “promote to III·A”→ new Enrollment per student, next year→ last year untouched = history

What changes from today: “department” becomes Program (Degree × Branch, programId); Class collapses into Section (with a year); Term becomes Semester; and two new tables — Attendance and InternalMark — land the two daily jobs we don't have yet.
